My Hometown - Ranikhet

Ranikhet (queen's Meadow) is a hill station and cantonment town, in the Indian state of Uttarakhand. Ranikhet is one of the most popular attractions in Uttarakhand that is blessed with some of the best scenic vistas and natural beauty. The name 'Ranikhet' was given by Queen Padmini who was the wife of King Sukhdev, the ruler of the state there. Seeing the beauty of Ranikhet, the king and queen were very impressed and decided to stay there.

Attractions in Ranikhet

In the valleys of this little town, one can hear the mellow sound of hundreds of bells and Himalayan forest thrush all day long. There are many best places to visit in Ranikhet that attract thousands of mountain lovers. some are them are listed below.

  • Ranikhet Golf Course

     One of the highest golf courses in Asia, the famous Upat Golf Course also known as Ranikhet Golf Course is situated only 6 km away from Ranikhet. It is located near Ghingari Khal and is looked after by the Kumaon Regiment.



  • KRC (Kumaon Regimental centre ) Museum

     KRC (Kumaon Regimental Centre) is a well-maintained museum that showcases the glory ,achievements and triumphs of the Kumaon Regiment of Indian Army. The museum was built in 1970's so as to preserve and exhibit the legacy and customs of the Kumaon Region of Uttarakhand State of India.



  • Haidakhan Babaji Temple

     The Haidakhan Babaji Temple is a spiritual retreat located in Ranikhet, Uttarakhand. The ashram is dedicated to the teachings and practices of Haidakhan Babaji, a reversed saint and yogi who is believed to be the incarnation of Lord Shiva by his followers.



  • Chaubatia Gardens

     The Chaubatia Gardens are perched at an altitude of 1,800 meters above sea level and sprawls over 600 acres of land. This place is renowned for the orchards laden with fruits like peaches, apples, apricots and many more.
